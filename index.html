Gaussian Splatting

Gaussian Splatting is a technique in computer graphics and imaging where 3D points are represented as Gaussian distributions instead of discrete points or polygons. Each point becomes a “splat” with spatial spread and orientation, offering a smooth, continuous representation of surfaces from sparse or noisy data.

This approach is especially useful in rendering 3D scenes from point clouds, volume visualization, and photorealistic reconstruction, providing natural blending and higher visual fidelity.
